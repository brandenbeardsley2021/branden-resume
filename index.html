Adyen Feb 2025 – Present · Payments Performance Manager
↔ Stripe Authorization Boost · Payments Intelligence

Authorization Boost needs a PM who can translate ML signal improvements into merchant revenue — and run the experiments that prove it.

01

Enterprise merchants with multi-processor stacks demand proof before switching. Adyen's Uplift product — like Stripe's Authorization Boost — only lands if the PM can design rigorous A/B tests against competing processors and turn the results into commercial decisions. In the first 8 weeks at Adyen, expanded the Protect portfolio to 30+ household-name brands and drove $15M+ in ARR through enterprise upsell. This is the current day-to-day.

The challenge at Adyen
  • No structured methodology to compare processors
  • Account teams couldn't run tests without specialist support
  • Competitors made unverifiable authorization claims
  • Product adoption stalled without third-party proof points
How it was solved
  • Designed controlled A/B test methodology with data science team — defined statistical significance thresholds, traffic splitting, and measurement windows
  • Built commercial enablement program so AM teams could configure and run processor comparison tests without specialist support
  • Created data-driven switching narratives — translating basis-point improvements into dollar-denominated merchant revenue impact
  • Advised 50+ enterprise merchants on global checkout optimization, including multi-acquirer routing and issuer-level auth strategy
Why it translates to Stripe
  • Authorization Boost faces the exact same merchant proof-point challenge
  • Multi-acquirer routing and issuer-level auth strategy maps to Stripe's intelligent routing and network token optimization
  • Enablement model maps directly to Stripe's Sales + PM dynamic
  • Building payment infrastructure that serves real-time predictions at scale — the same architectural challenge

Chime 2019 – 2021 · Fraud Operations & Strategy
↔ Stripe Radar · Operational Scale

Scaling fraud operations from pilot to production requires a PM who has built the systems, not just the models.

04

ML models don't catch fraud alone — they need operational infrastructure: investigators who action alerts, case routing logic that scales, accuracy measurement that catches drift, and forecasting that keeps headcount ahead of volume. This is the operational backbone that makes Radar effective at scale.

The challenge at Chime
  • Fraud investigation was ad-hoc — no structured case management or routing
  • No forecasting to predict dispute volume or staffing needs
  • Case accuracy unmeasured — no SLAs, no feedback loops
  • Investigation backlog growing faster than headcount
How it was solved
  • Designed case routing logic and SLA frameworks — defined what "good" looked like before scaling
  • Launched a 150+ investigator fraud ops site from pilot, hiring and training in partnership with the operations team
  • Built dispute forecasting models that predicted volume 30 days out, clearing 30% of case backlog in 6 months
  • Defined accuracy measurement frameworks that sustained 95%+ case accuracy at scale
Why it translates to Stripe
  • Radar's effectiveness depends on the operational layer beneath the ML — the same layer built at Chime
  • Forecasting dispute volume is directly applicable to Radar capacity planning
  • Scaling from pilot to 150+ people is the kind of operational judgment this role requires
  • Accuracy frameworks translate directly to Radar's precision/recall monitoring
